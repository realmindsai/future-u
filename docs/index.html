AI as a leg up — not a search box.

Tonight you'll use AI as a study coach, writing coach, and decision partner — so you can move faster, ask better questions, and turn your real life experience into opportunity.

You're already translating between worlds every day — your language at home and English at school, your parents' expectations and your own. AI is just one more tool for doing that translation faster, and on your terms.

If you want to bring your full self into the prompt — your name, your family, your community — the AI will work better for you. If you want to keep it generic, that's fine too. Your call.

The three we'll do tonight

Three exercises, ~15 minutes each. Phones are fine. Try them on something real to you.

Tonight · A 01

Explain this like I'm me

AI translates hard material into your world.

 Tonight · B 03

Cover letter from my actual life

AI sees value in experience you might not know how to name.

 Tonight · C 05

Ask better questions than Google

AI teaches you how to think, not just what to look up.

One rule before you start

Don't put private information into AI. No home address, passwords, Centrelink or customer numbers, medical details, immigration details — or anything that could hurt you if it got copied somewhere else.

What you'll leave with

  • Two or three workflows you can try tomorrow
  • This site — ten prompts to come back to
