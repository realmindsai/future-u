Take-home

08 · Side hustle / community idea generator

AGI moment: AI can help you move from idea to prototype — fast, cheap, real.

The hook

You don't need to be 30 with a business degree to start something. You need a small idea, a first customer, and 50 dollars. AI can help you find all three.

Try this prompt

I'm a young person in [suburb / community, e.g. Sunshine, west of Melbourne]. I have these skills: [list 3–5 — e.g. cutting hair, fixing phones, editing video, helping with English homework, baking, fashion, music, sport coaching]. I care about: [pick a few — music, sport, hair, food, tutoring, clothing, tech, culture, community]. Give me 5 small project or side-hustle ideas I could test with less than $50. Be specific. No 'become an influencer' rubbish. Each idea should have: - who would pay or use it - what I'd actually make or do - the first weekend's work

Then go deep on one

Pick the idea I'd be best at: [pick one]. Create: 1. A name 2. A first customer (specific person or type — who would I call this week?) 3. Flyer text (5 lines max — for a social post or a printed A4) 4. A simple budget for the first month, assuming I have $50 5. The first three actions I should take in the next 7 days

Why it lands

Imagination is cheap. Prototypes are the new flex. AI turns a vague "I should start a thing" into something you can show your mate by Sunday.

Family + community is an asset, not a constraint

A lot of the best small businesses in Melbourne run on community trust before they run on marketing. If your skill is something your own community already needs (hair, food, tutoring, translating, event setup, sound, photography for weddings and naming days), start there.

Add to the prompt: "My first customers might be from my own community — South Sudanese-Australian / [your community]. Build the idea so it works there first, and can grow out."

The honest reality check

Ask AI to push back on you:

Now stress-test this idea. What could go wrong? Where would I lose money? What would make me give up in week 2? Be straight with me.
