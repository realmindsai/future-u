Take-home

07 · Build me a personal study coach

AGI moment: AI can become an adaptive, one-on-one tutor — the thing most of us couldn't afford.

The hook

A tutor that explains it your way. Quizzes you. Notices what you don't get. Doesn't make you feel stupid when you ask the same question three times. Sits with you at 10pm the night before. It exists now.

Try this prompt

Teach me this topic: [SUBJECT / TOPIC, e.g. quadratic equations / cell division / how the federal election works / supply and demand] First explain it simply, like I'm catching up after missing class. Then quiz me with 5 questions, one at a time. Wait for my answer before going to the next. If I get something wrong, explain the mistake without making me feel stupid. At the end, tell me which parts I solid on, and which parts I need to come back to.

Then revise tomorrow

Make a memory trick (mnemonic or story) for the parts I got wrong. Then write me a 10-minute revision plan for tomorrow morning, with the smallest first step.

Why it lands

Adaptive one-on-one tutoring used to cost $60–100 an hour. It's free now if you know how to ask. Knowing how to ask is the new advantage.

Tricks that make it actually useful

  • Tell it what you already know. "I already get X, just don't get Y" saves time.
  • Tell it how you learn. "Use diagrams in words." "Use real examples." "Compare it to something I know."
  • Don't pretend you got it. Say "explain it again, differently" as many times as you need.
  • Get it to test you, not just lecture. Testing is what makes it stick.

If your textbook is in English and your home language isn't

"Explain this concept in plain English. Then explain it again in [language] for me to send to my [parent/sibling/cousin] who's also studying it. Keep the technical terms in English but the explanation in [language]."
