01 · Explain this like I'm me

AGI moment: AI can translate difficult material into your world.

The hook

You missed three weeks of a subject. Or it's in your weakest language. Or you nearly get it but can't quite say it. Or there's a government form, a job ad, a rental notice, a scholarship description full of words designed to make you feel stupid. Watch this.

Try this prompt

Explain this to me like I'm 17, smart, but new to this topic. Use examples from everyday life. Then give me the 5 things I actually need to remember. [Paste the hard paragraph here]

Then follow up

Now explain it using an example from [football / hairdressing / music / gaming / church / family business / my local community].

Why it lands

It shows AI as a bridge between formal systems and lived experience. The textbook version doesn't change. You get a version made for you.

What you can paste in

  • A paragraph from school, TAFE, or uni notes you didn't follow
  • A job ad with jargon you don't recognise
  • A scholarship description
  • A bank, Centrelink, or rental email (with private numbers stripped out)
  • An article someone sent you that you didn't finish

Try it on something different

Once you've done it for one thing, do it for the same paragraph but in a different style. Ask for an example "in the voice of a 65-year-old auntie" or "as a TikTok script". You'll see how flexible AI is — and how much control you have over it.

Reminder: Strip out any private information before you paste — no Centrelink/customer numbers, no addresses, no full names of people who haven't said it's OK.
