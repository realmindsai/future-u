Take-home

04 · Interview coach that doesn't judge me

AGI moment: AI can simulate a high-pressure situation safely.

The hook

You don't get many chances to practise being interviewed. And the cost of getting it wrong is the job you wanted. AI gives you private rehearsal. Mess up as many times as you want. Nobody sees.

Try this prompt

Act as a friendly but serious interviewer for a [retail / admin / apprentice / youth worker / hospitality / pick yours] job. Ask me one question at a time. After each answer, give me feedback and a stronger version of my answer in my own voice. Don't be soft on me — but don't be mean either. Start with question 1.

How to use it

  • Answer out loud, like a real interview. Then type a short version of what you said.
  • Read the AI's "stronger version" out loud once. Does it still sound like you?
  • If it sounds fake — push back: "That's too formal, give me a version a normal 18-year-old would actually say."
  • Keep going for 5–10 questions. The last three are the ones that change you.

Why it lands

Private rehearsal. No embarrassment, immediate improvement. The thing that used to need a paid coach or an older sibling who'd done it before.

Harder mode

Now ask me the hard questions employers actually ask. The ones young people get tripped up on. Include questions about gaps, why I left somewhere, what I'd do if a customer was rude, and what I'd do if I made a mistake.
