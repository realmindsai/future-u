Tonight · C Decision sparring

05 · Ask better questions than Google

AGI moment: AI isn't just an answer machine — it can teach you how to think.

The hook

AI is rubbish at telling you what to do. It's brilliant at helping you think. Watch the difference.

Try this prompt

I'm trying to decide whether to [DECISION — e.g. take the job, switch course, move out, start the side thing, study nursing vs trade vs design]. Don't answer yet. Ask me 10 questions that would help me make a better decision. Include questions about money, lifestyle, strengths, stress, family expectations, and future opportunities. I'll answer them one at a time.

Then close the loop

Based on my answers, give me 3 possible pathways and the trade-offs of each. Don't tell me which to pick. Tell me what I'd be choosing in each one.

Why it lands

This is the mindset shift. Stop searching. Start talking. AI as a thinking partner — not an oracle, not Google.

Bring your full self if you want

Most decision-making advice you'll read online treats "what does your family think" as a problem to overcome. It's not. For a lot of us, family and community input is a legitimate part of how good decisions get made.

Try adding to the prompt:
  • "Treat 'what would be good for my family and community' as a real input, not a hurdle to overcome."
  • "My parents want me to do [X]. I want [Y]. Help me think about both honestly — including what's actually behind their preference."

The question that surprises you

After AI gives you the 10 questions, before you answer them, scan them. Which one made you flinch? Which one made you go "oh I haven't thought about that"? That's the one to start with.

What it's not

  • It's not a therapist. If something's heavy, talk to a real person.
  • It's not a fortune teller. It doesn't know the future. It's a sparring partner for now.
  • It's not your boss or your parent. You get to disagree with it.
