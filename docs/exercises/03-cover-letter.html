Tonight · B The "seen" moment

03 · Cover letter from my actual life

AGI moment: AI can see value in experience you might not know how to name.

The hook

Most of you have done things adults pay other people to do.

  • Looking after younger siblings is project management.
  • Translating for your family is professional language work.
  • Holding the house when someone gets sick is operations.
  • Sorting an argument between cousins is mediation.
  • Running the youth team at church or sport is leadership.

The job market just doesn't know how to read it. Let's fix that.

Try this prompt

I'm applying for [JOB / OPPORTUNITY]. I'm [AGE] and I haven't had a 'professional' job, but I've done these things in my life: 1. [e.g. caring for my younger siblings since I was 14] 2. [e.g. translating for my parents at appointments] 3. [e.g. running the youth team at church / managing the family budget / holding it together when mum got sick / pick three real ones] Show me how each of those is actually a skill an employer values. Don't exaggerate. Use confident but honest language. Then write me a short paragraph for my application.

Why it lands

This is the moment. Watch faces when the AI names back what you've already done as something the world will pay for. The skill the room doesn't know it has.

If you want to bring your full self

You come from a tradition where people compose songs about who they are — their family, where they're from, what they've done. A cover letter is the boring Australian version of that. Make yours less boring.

Try adding to the prompt:
  • "My name is [X]. In my family / language it means [Y]. Use that as a thread if it fits."
  • "My community is [South Sudanese-Australian in Melbourne's west / wherever you're from]. Don't make it the main thing. Just let it be part of who's writing this."
Skip this if it's not your vibe. Your call.

Now sharpen it

Make it shorter, more confident, and less corporate. Read it again. Cut any sentence that sounds like a fake adult wrote it.

Push back when it gets it wrong

If the AI inflates something or makes you sound like a LinkedIn robot, tell it so:

  • "Cut the line about 'passionate self-starter' — sounds fake."
  • "You made me sound 30. I'm 17. Pull it back."
  • "Keep my voice. The way I'd actually say it to a real person."
Don't paste real personal details from yourself or your family (full names, addresses, Medicare numbers). Use first names or initials. AI doesn't need the rest.
