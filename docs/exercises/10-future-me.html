Take-home

10 · Future-me simulator

AGI moment: AI can help you imagine a possible self — and walk you backwards into concrete steps.

The hook

Most "what do you want to be" conversations are useless. Either you don't know (so it makes you feel bad), or you do know (and they don't tell you how to get there). This one is different — because it asks you the questions, then works backwards into something you can start in 30 days.

Try this prompt

Interview me about the kind of life I want in 3 years. Ask one question at a time. Wait for my answer before the next. Cover: where I'd be living, who I'd be with, what I'd be spending my days on, money, study/work, what I'd be proud of, what would feel different from now. After 8 questions, write me a realistic future profile of me at that age. Then work backwards into a 30-day plan to make the first step real.

Then make it real

Now make it more realistic if I have setbacks, money pressure, family pressure, or I lose motivation. Don't be soft. Show me what to do when it gets hard. What's the smallest thing I can still do on a bad week?

Why it lands

Connects aspiration to action. No fake positivity — the constraints are part of the plan, not something the plan pretends doesn't exist.

Use it once a year

Try this exercise once a year — your birthday, January 1, the start of a new term, the end of a job. Compare what you wrote a year ago to where you actually are. That comparison is the data. AI can run it with you.

Here's the future profile I wrote 12 months ago: [paste]. Here's where I actually am now: [paste]. What changed? What got harder than I thought? What got easier? What's the new plan for the next 12?

One thing to remember

The point of this exercise is not to lock you in. It's to give you a draft of a self you might become — so you can edit it, push back on it, and start one small thing this week.
