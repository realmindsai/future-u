Take-home

06 · Translate formal language into power language

AGI moment: AI can help you navigate institutions without losing power.

The hook

A lot of formal language is designed to make you feel small or to make you act before you've understood. Centrelink letters, school policy, rental notices, scholarship criteria, hospital paperwork. The trick most people miss: you can slow it down. You can ask what it actually means. You can answer in a way that protects you.

Try this prompt

Here's a letter/email/notice I got: [Paste the message — strip out personal numbers like Centrelink CRN, customer IDs, your address] Translate this into plain English. Tell me what they are really asking for. Tell me what action I should take. Tell me what I should NOT ignore. Tell me if anything in it sounds wrong or worth questioning.

Then write the reply

Now draft a polite reply that protects my interests. Don't sound aggressive. Don't sound scared. Sound like someone who knows their rights and asks clear questions.

Why it lands

Marginalised young people often lose power at the interface with institutions. This gives you a way to slow the system down, understand it, and respond in your own time.

If your parents need it too

Lots of us translate for our parents and grandparents — at appointments, in emails, on forms. AI can help you do that better.

Try adding:
  • "Now write a version I could read out to my grandmother in plain English she'd understand. Keep the important parts. Skip the legal-sounding stuff."
  • "Translate this into [language] in everyday speech, not formal writing."

Use it on

  • Centrelink-style letters
  • School or TAFE policy emails
  • Job ad selection criteria (the dot-points you have to "address")
  • Scholarship criteria
  • Medical appointment instructions
  • Tenancy / real-estate messages
  • Bank emails about your account
One thing to remember: AI gives you a starting point — not legal advice. If something looks serious (eviction, immigration, debt collection, fines, threats), call a free service: Youthlaw, Legal Aid, or your local community legal centre. AI can help you prepare for that call. It shouldn't replace it.
