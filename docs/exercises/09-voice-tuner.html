Take-home

09 · Sound professional without sounding fake

AGI moment: AI helps you code-switch without losing yourself.

The hook

You already code-switch every day. Different voice with your mates, with your aunties, with the teacher, with the cousin who only speaks at home. Professional is just one more voice. It's a learnable surface skill — not a measure of how smart you are.

Try this prompt

Here's a message I want to send: [Paste your rough draft. E.g. 'hey just wondering if u got any jobs going i need work thanks'] Rewrite it in three versions: 1. Friendly and natural 2. Professional 3. Confident but not arrogant Keep my voice. Don't make me sound like a corporate robot. Don't add things I wouldn't actually say.

Pick + adjust

Version 2 is closest but it's a bit stiff. Loosen it up. Cut anything that sounds like a fake adult. Keep it under 60 words.

Why it lands

Professional communication is a learnable surface skill, not a measure of intelligence. Some of the smartest people you know sound rough on email. The trick is knowing how to put on the jacket when you need to, and take it off when you don't.

Use it for

  • A message to a potential employer or boss
  • An email to a teacher / TAFE staff / uni admin
  • A LinkedIn message
  • A complaint that needs to be taken seriously
  • Asking for something — money owed, a reference, a meeting

Keep your voice — don't erase it

If AI's version doesn't sound like you, push back:

  • "You added 'I am writing to inquire'. I'd never say that. Cut it."
  • "Use shorter sentences. The way I actually talk."
  • "Keep the bit where I said why it matters to me. That's the real part."
